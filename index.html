Sophie is a woodwind multi-instrumentalist based in London, originally from Cork, Ireland. She records sessions, performs in theatre shows and plays early music.

Remote recording

Sophie records existing lines or can help to compose woodwind parts. There are boundless possibilities for new sounds with woodwind instruments, especially when layering different lines together to create unique and exciting textures.

She can record any of these instruments:

  • Clarinets

    • Clarinet
    • Bass clarinet
    • Eb clarinet

  • Flutes

    • Flute
    • Alto flute
    • Piccolo

  • Recorders

    • Contrabass to sopranino
    • Renaissance, Medieval, Baroque, Modern

  • World Flutes

    • Irish flute
    • Bansuri (Indian)
    • Sáo trúc (Vietnamese)
    • Dizi (Chinese)

  • Saxophones

    • Soprano saxophone
    • Alto saxophone
    • Tenor saxophone
    • Baritone saxophone

She can also offer any combination of instruments by bringing in colleagues from the industry to collaborate with on a project. This negates any disparities when recording musicians separately.

Sophie's Career

Sophie performs primarily as a musician for West End shows, alongside being very active in the UK and European early music scenes. The best thing about West End shows is the variety of instruments and styles that she has to play on a daily basis, all to the highest professional standard. Sometimes, her musical theatre and early music branches colide, as with upcoming musical Treason, which has its first taste of performance in the Cadogan Hall this year, streamed online on 12-14 February.

Lux Musicae

Sophie is a founding member of the mixed consort Lux Musicae London, formed to musically explore the transition from the Renaissance to the Early Baroque.
